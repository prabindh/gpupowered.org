In order to be realistic and as close to the physical skin view of their natural environment, graphics rendering needs to account for what is termed as "sub-surface scattering", needed because of the physical fact that human skin is ~translucent in the upper layer. This is in addition to the usual Reflectance factors that are commonly being used. Most of the below algorithms, use a shadow map and use it for further processing. Since hair is closely associated with skin (even small hair), it is important to see both of these together.
